Tokyo Station Character Street and Akiba Merch Day

A merch-heavy route that starts polished and official at Tokyo Station, then shifts into denser collector energy in Akihabara.

Photo highlights

Suggested flow

  • Start at First Avenue Tokyo Station, with Tokyo Character Street as the main opening block.
  • This works especially well if you like official-shop energy, station exclusives, and a broad spread of character brands in one place.
  • Move to Akihabara in the afternoon and anchor the second half in Akihabara Radio Kaikan, whose building directory includes stores like AmiAmi, K-BOOKS, Lashinbang, Kaiyodo, and Yellow Submarine.
  • Use the Tokyo Station side for cleaner official merch and the Akihabara side for secondhand, figures, hobby goods, and harder-to-predict finds.

Opening-time considerations

  • The English First Avenue Tokyo Station guide lists Tokyo Character Street as 10:00 to 20:30.
  • Akihabara Radio Kaikan lists general building hours as 10:00 to 20:00.
  • This is best as a full daytime plan rather than a late-start evening browse.

Why this is a fit

  • Good if you want one anime/game merch day that is broader than a single franchise or single district.
  • One of the best plans for people who enjoy comparing official retail with resale/hobby store stock.

Extra planning notes

  • Best for: fandom-heavy days where shopping, pop-ups, and current collabs are a real part of the fun rather than an afterthought.
  • Pacing tip: set a budget ceiling early, because these days are easy to extend by hours once you start finding stores or limited goods you like.
  • Easy adjustment: if the current event or merch situation looks weak, keep the area but pivot toward cafes, arcades, or one neighboring district instead.
