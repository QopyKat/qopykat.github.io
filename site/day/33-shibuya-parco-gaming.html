Shibuya PARCO Gaming Brands Day

Photo highlights

Suggested flow

  • Put Shibuya PARCO at the center of the day.
  • Prioritize the most relevant official stores instead of trying to browse everything:
  • Nintendo TOKYO
  • Pokémon Center SHIBUYA
  • CAPCOM STORE TOKYO
  • If you are also into card culture, keep POKEMON CARD LOUNGE context in mind as an optional separate stop nearby rather than forcing it into the same schedule.
  • End with dinner or drinks in Shibuya instead of leaving immediately after shopping.

Opening-time considerations

  • Nintendo TOKYO and Pokémon Center SHIBUYA are both listed on the 6F cluster with 10:00 to 21:00.
  • CAPCOM STORE TOKYO is also on the same Shibuya PARCO floor cluster and is easiest to combine in one block.
  • This area can get crowded enough that it is smarter to start earlier than on most shopping-heavy Tokyo days.

Why this is a fit

  • One of the best pure gaming days in Tokyo.
  • Very efficient if you care more about official Nintendo/Pokemon/Capcom goods than random resale hunting.

Extra planning notes

  • Best for: fandom-heavy days where shopping, pop-ups, and current collabs are a real part of the fun rather than an afterthought.
  • Pacing tip: set a budget ceiling early, because these days are easy to extend by hours once you start finding stores or limited goods you like.
  • Easy adjustment: if the current event or merch situation looks weak, keep the area but pivot toward cafes, arcades, or one neighboring district instead.
