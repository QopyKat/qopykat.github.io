Okutama and Mt. Mitake Day

More mountain atmosphere than Takao, with better “escape from Tokyo” feeling but more transit.

Photo highlights

Suggested flow

  • Use the official Okutama tourism site and Mt. Mitake info to decide whether to do shrine-focused Mitake or a gorge walk.
  • Keep this as a single-objective day; do not over-stack too many trail sections.

Opening-time considerations

  • This requires an earlier start than Takao.
  • Best skipped in heavy rain or if trail surfaces may be icy.

Why this is a fit

  • Stronger wilderness feel than the easier Tokyo nature options.

Extra planning notes

  • Best for: using a good weather window well and committing to one regional area instead of trying to combine too many side trips.
  • Pacing tip: start earlier than on Tokyo city days and keep at least one part of the itinerary skippable in case transit, weather, or crowds slow you down.
  • Easy adjustment: if conditions look poor in the morning, replace this with one of the indoor-heavy Tokyo plans and save the trip for a clearer day.
