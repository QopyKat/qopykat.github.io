Water Bus and Small Worlds Future-Tokyo Day

A highly different day that mixes Tokyo-from-the-water with a miniature museum and a slightly surreal Ariake/Odaiba mood.

Photo highlights

Suggested flow

  • Start with the Asakusa water bus / cruise context from GO TOKYO and use the river-to-bay transition as the first half of the day.
  • Pair that with SMALL WORLDS Miniature Museum, which is large, indoor, and genuinely unusual by Tokyo standards.
  • This day works best if you lean into the contrast between old-river Tokyo and futuristic/artificial waterfront Tokyo.
  • End with an Ariake or Odaiba dinner if the weather is clear, or just head back once the indoor part is done.

Opening-time considerations

  • SMALL WORLDS currently lists 09:00 to 19:00, with last admission around 18:00.
  • Water bus schedules are the most important timing constraint here; confirm the specific sailing you want before committing the rest of the route.

Why this is a fit

  • One of the most different-feeling days in the whole set.
  • Good for bad weather if the cruise portion is still running and you want a mostly indoor backup after it.

Extra planning notes

  • Best for: when you want one day in the trip to feel genuinely unusual or unlike standard first-time Tokyo sightseeing.
  • Pacing tip: let the unusual anchor be the point of the day and resist overloading it with unrelated stops just to make the itinerary feel fuller.
  • Easy adjustment: if the main anchor turns out to be shorter than expected, add a nearby cafe, neighborhood walk, or early dinner rather than rushing across the city.
