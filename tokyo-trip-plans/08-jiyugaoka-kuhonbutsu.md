# 08. Jiyugaoka, Kuhonbutsu, and Tamagawa Stroll Day

Short summary: Residential Tokyo, sweets, independent shops, temple calm, and a riverside reset.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,500 to 6,500`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 500 to 1,000`
  - Food, sweets, and other: `JPY 2,000 to 5,500`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `25 to 40 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `private railway + walking`
  - Rough route: go `southwest` toward `Jiyugaoka`, stay mostly on foot between Jiyugaoka and Kuhonbutsu, then optionally continue farther south toward the Tamagawa river edge
- Map links:
  - [Jiyugaoka](https://maps.google.com/?q=Jiyugaoka)
  - [Kuhonbutsu Joshinji Temple](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kuhonbutsu+Joshinji+Temple)

Suggested flow:
- Spend late morning in Jiyugaoka for coffee, pastry shops, and side-street wandering.
- Walk or short-hop to [Kuhonbutsu Joshinji Temple](https://kuhombutsu.jp/).
- If the weather is crisp and dry, finish with a Tamagawa riverside walk.

Opening-time considerations:
- This is better as a slow-start day; the main appeal is shopping and strolling rather than early-opening sights.
- Keep the riverside section for daylight.

Why this is a fit:
- Good palate-cleanser between busier central-city days.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
