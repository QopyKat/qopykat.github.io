# 30. New Year's Eve Night-Owl Plan

Short summary: Treat December 31 as a flexible, late-start day that saves energy for the night.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 4,000 to 12,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 400 to 900`
  - Food, drinks, and other: `JPY 3,600 to 11,100`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 35 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + walking`, with possible late-night crowd movement on foot
  - Rough route: keep the daytime light, then move toward one `central/southwest` shrine, temple, or countdown area instead of crossing the city repeatedly at midnight
- Map links:
  - [Meiji Jingu area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Meiji+Jingu)
  - [Zojoji area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Zojoji)

Suggested flow:
- Keep the day light: coffee, neighborhood wandering, early dinner, then one late-night temple/shrine choice or a hotel/bar countdown.
- If you want bells and a traditional atmosphere, research current late-night access rules closer to the trip.
- If you want comfort, make this your easiest dining-reservation night and skip major crowds.

Opening-time considerations:
- Many museums and some shops will close early or already be closed.
- Transport normally continues, but verify holiday timetables when the trip is booked.

Why this is a fit:
- Works best if you lean into the holiday rhythm instead of trying to sightsee normally.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: special dates when atmosphere matters more than maximizing attractions.
- Pacing tip: protect one reservation or one main holiday moment, then keep the rest of the day deliberately flexible.
- Easy adjustment: if closures or crowds become annoying, simplify the plan into one neighborhood, one meal booking, and one evening activity.
