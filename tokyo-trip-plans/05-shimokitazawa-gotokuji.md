# 05. Shimokitazawa and Gotokuji Day

Short summary: Vintage-heavy neighborhood wandering with a small temple detour and strong bar/cafe options.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 7,500` before shopping
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 450 to 900`
  - Food, cafes, and other: `JPY 2,550 to 6,600` before shopping
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `20 to 35 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `private railway + walking`
  - Rough route: go `west/southwest` toward `Gotokuji`, then continue by local line or short walk into `Shimokitazawa`, staying there through the evening before returning direct
- Map links:
  - [Shimokitazawa](https://maps.google.com/?q=Shimokitazawa)
  - [Gotokuji Temple](https://maps.google.com/?q=Gotokuji+Temple)

Suggested flow:
- Begin at [Gotokuji Temple](https://gotokuji.jp/) for the quieter morning.
- Move into Shimokitazawa around lunch using [GO TOKYO's neighborhood guide](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/destinations/western-tokyo/shimokitazawa/index.html).
- Spend the afternoon on vintage shops, record stores, coffee, and side streets.
- Return at night for izakaya or bars rather than treating it as only a daytime shopping stop.

Opening-time considerations:
- Most Shimokitazawa retail opens from late morning to noon.
- Best day structure is temple first, lunch second, shopping third, bars last.

Why this is a fit:
- High hit rate for neighborhoods, wandering, food, and nightlife without clubbing.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
