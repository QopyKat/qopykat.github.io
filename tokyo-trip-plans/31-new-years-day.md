# 31. New Year's Day Quiet-City Plan

Short summary: January 1 should be treated as a limited-options day built around atmosphere, shrines, walks, and whatever is confirmed open.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,000 to 6,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 400 to 900`
  - Food, snacks, and other: `JPY 1,600 to 5,100`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 35 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + relaxed walking`
  - Rough route: keep the route compact around one `bay`, `garden`, or `shrine` zone and avoid assuming you can easily improvise across many neighborhoods on January 1
- Map links:
  - [Hamarikyu Gardens area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Hamarikyu+Gardens)
  - [Tokyo Bay / Odaiba promenade](https://maps.google.com/?q=Odaiba)

Suggested flow:
- Choose one of three modes:
  - shrine/temple visit very early or very late
  - bay walk and relaxed food plan
  - onsen or ryokan overnight extension
- Keep backup snacks and cash.
- Do not overcommit to independent shops or museums on this date.

Opening-time considerations:
- This is the highest-risk closure day in the whole trip.
- Confirm everything again once exact dates are known.

Why this is a fit:
- Realistic for the actual holiday pattern in Japan.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: special dates when atmosphere matters more than maximizing attractions.
- Pacing tip: protect one reservation or one main holiday moment, then keep the rest of the day deliberately flexible.
- Easy adjustment: if closures or crowds become annoying, simplify the plan into one neighborhood, one meal booking, and one evening activity.
