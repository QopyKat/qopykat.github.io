# 13. Akihabara, Kanda Myojin, and Retro Games Day

Short summary: A more selective Akihabara day focused on games, arcades, and anime culture without staying in the loudest blocks all day.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 9,000` plus game shopping
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 350 to 700`
  - Food, arcades, and other: `JPY 2,650 to 8,300` plus game shopping
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 25 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + walking`
  - Rough route: go `north/northeast` toward `Kanda/Akihabara`, start at the shrine slightly uphill from the main strip, then descend into `Akihabara` and stay there on foot
- Map links:
  - [Akihabara Electric Town](https://maps.google.com/?q=Akihabara+Electric+Town)
  - [Kanda Myojin](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kanda+Myojin)

Suggested flow:
- Start at [Kanda Myojin](https://www.kandamyoujin.or.jp/en/) while the area is quieter.
- Move into Akihabara for retro game stores, arcades, and themed shopping.
- Keep one dedicated arcade session instead of constantly stopping in every building.
- Escape for dinner in nearby Kanda or Jimbocho if Akihabara feels too hectic by evening.

Opening-time considerations:
- Most retail opens around late morning.
- Shrine first, stores second, arcades third is the cleanest flow.

Why this is a fit:
- Best core games/anime day without becoming all merch and no atmosphere.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
