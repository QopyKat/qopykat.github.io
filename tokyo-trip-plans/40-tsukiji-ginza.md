# 40. Tsukiji Breakfast and Ginza Day

Short summary: Start with Tokyo market energy, then shift into calmer architecture, food-floor browsing, and a polished central-Tokyo finish.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 8,500`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 300 to 700`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 2,700 to 7,800`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 30 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway/train + compact walking`
  - Rough route: head `southeast` to `Tsukiji` for the morning, walk north and west through `Hamarikyu or Tsukiji Hongwanji` territory if desired, then finish in `Ginza`
- Map links:
  - [Tsukiji Outer Market](https://maps.google.com/?q=Tsukiji+Outer+Market)
  - [Ginza](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ginza)

Suggested flow:
- Let Chris Broad's [6 Must Try Japanese Dishes in Tokyo | Tsukiji Street Food](https://www.abroadinjapan.com/content-276-6-must-try-japanese-dishes-in-tokyo-tsukiji-street-food) and [Tokyo's Freshest Sushi | Tsukiji Fishmarket](https://www.abroadinjapan.com/content-52-tokyos-freshest-sushitsukiji-fishmarket) set the tone: go early, eat well, and don't overcomplicate it.
- Use [GO TOKYO's Tsukiji guide](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/destinations/central-tokyo/tsukiji/index.html) as the structure for the area, especially if you want to pair the market with Hamarikyu or Tsukiji Hongwanji.
- Treat Ginza as the softer second half for cafes, depachika browsing, stationery, or a more refined dinner.

Opening-time considerations:
- The whole point of this day is the morning, so do not start late.
- Tsukiji is best early, while Ginza becomes more useful from late morning into evening.

Why this is a fit:
- It matches Chris's food-first Tokyo advice and adds a high-payoff central day that is easy to personalize.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: travelers who like food-led days more than shrine-hopping or museum stacking.
- Pacing tip: choose either a heavier breakfast or a nicer Ginza dinner, not both, if you want to avoid food fatigue.
- Easy adjustment: if weather turns bad, cut the park/walking layer and make this a market-plus-department-store day.
