# 33. Shibuya PARCO Gaming Brands Day

Short summary: A focused gaming-retail day centered on Shibuya PARCO's unusually dense official stores.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 9,000` before shopping
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 350 to 700`
  - Food, arcades/cafes, and other: `JPY 2,650 to 8,300` before shopping
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 30 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + one-district walking`
  - Rough route: head `west/southwest` to `Shibuya`, keep the core of the day inside the `PARCO` and nearby cluster, then finish locally rather than relocating
- Map links:
  - [Shibuya PARCO](https://maps.google.com/?q=Shibuya+PARCO)
  - [Pokémon Center SHIBUYA](https://maps.google.com/?q=Pokemon+Center+Shibuya)
  - [Nintendo TOKYO](https://maps.google.com/?q=Nintendo+TOKYO)

Suggested flow:
- Put [Shibuya PARCO](https://shibuya.parco.jp/) at the center of the day.
- Prioritize the most relevant official stores instead of trying to browse everything:
  - [Nintendo TOKYO](https://shibuya.parco.jp/shop/detail/?cd=025793)
  - [Pokémon Center SHIBUYA](https://www.pokemon.co.jp/shop/en/pokecen/shibuya/)
  - [CAPCOM STORE TOKYO](https://shibuya.parco.jp/shop/detail/?cd=025792)
- If you are also into card culture, keep [POKEMON CARD LOUNGE context](https://corporate.pokemon.co.jp/en/topics/detail/122.html) in mind as an optional separate stop nearby rather than forcing it into the same schedule.
- End with dinner or drinks in Shibuya instead of leaving immediately after shopping.

Opening-time considerations:
- Nintendo TOKYO and Pokémon Center SHIBUYA are both listed on the 6F cluster with `10:00 to 21:00`.
- CAPCOM STORE TOKYO is also on the same Shibuya PARCO floor cluster and is easiest to combine in one block.
- This area can get crowded enough that it is smarter to start earlier than on most shopping-heavy Tokyo days.

Why this is a fit:
- One of the best pure gaming days in Tokyo.
- Very efficient if you care more about official Nintendo/Pokemon/Capcom goods than random resale hunting.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: fandom-heavy days where shopping, pop-ups, and current collabs are a real part of the fun rather than an afterthought.
- Pacing tip: set a budget ceiling early, because these days are easy to extend by hours once you start finding stores or limited goods you like.
- Easy adjustment: if the current event or merch situation looks weak, keep the area but pivot toward cafes, arcades, or one neighboring district instead.
