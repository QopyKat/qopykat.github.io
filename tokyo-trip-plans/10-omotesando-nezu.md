# 10. Omotesando and Nezu Museum Day

Short summary: Design-forward shopping streets anchored by one of Tokyo's best compact museums and gardens.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,500 to 9,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 350 to 700`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 3,150 to 8,300`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 25 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway + walking`
  - Rough route: travel `west/southwest` to `Omotesando`, anchor on the museum first, then keep the rest of the route as a walkable loop through adjacent Aoyama streets
- Map links:
  - [Nezu Museum](https://maps.google.com/?q=Nezu+Museum)
  - [Omotesando](https://maps.google.com/?q=Omotesando)

Suggested flow:
- Visit [Nezu Museum](https://www.nezu-muse.or.jp/en/) first, then use the museum garden as the calmest part of the day.
- Spend the rest of the afternoon on Omotesando and adjacent Aoyama streets.
- Save this for a weekday if possible; weekends are notably busier.

Opening-time considerations:
- Nezu Museum is typically a daytime museum with a weekly closure day; build the day around that fixed window.
- This is one of the easiest plans to shorten if you want an early dinner somewhere else.

Why this is a fit:
- Strong architecture/culture balance with very little transit overhead.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
