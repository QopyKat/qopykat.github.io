# 37. Tokiwaso Manga History Day

Short summary: A manga-history day that feels very different from anime shopping districts, with more cultural context and less sensory overload.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,000 to 5,500`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 500 to 1,000`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 1,500 to 4,500`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `30 to 50 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `JR/subway/private railway + walking`
  - Rough route: head `northwest` toward the `Toshima/Shiinamachi` side, keep the day centered on the museum and immediate neighborhood, then return direct
- Map links:
  - [Tokiwaso Manga Museum](https://maps.google.com/?q=Tokiwaso+Manga+Museum)
  - [Shiinamachi area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Shiinamachi+Station)
  - [Minami-Nagasaki / Toshima area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Minami-Nagasaki+Tokyo)

Suggested flow:
- Build the day around the [Tokiwaso Manga Museum](https://tokiwasomm.jp/en/), which reconstructs the famous apartment complex associated with major postwar manga creators.
- Keep the surrounding neighborhood as part of the experience rather than rushing back to busier Tokyo districts.
- If you want a second manga-related stop, keep it small and context-driven rather than turning the day into another merch route.
- Good add-on day if the big anime/gaming days start to blur together.

Opening-time considerations:
- The [official English museum page](https://tokiwasomm.jp/en/) notes `10:00 to 18:00`, last admission around `17:30`, and priority for reservations.
- The museum is closed on Mondays or the following day if Monday is a holiday, plus exhibition-change periods.

Why this is a fit:
- Stronger for manga history and atmosphere than for shopping.
- Feels more distinctive and reflective than Akihabara or Ikebukuro.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: when you want one day in the trip to feel genuinely unusual or unlike standard first-time Tokyo sightseeing.
- Pacing tip: let the unusual anchor be the point of the day and resist overloading it with unrelated stops just to make the itinerary feel fuller.
- Easy adjustment: if the main anchor turns out to be shorter than expected, add a nearby cafe, neighborhood walk, or early dinner rather than rushing across the city.
