# 22. Okutama and Mt. Mitake Day

Short summary: More mountain atmosphere than Takao, with better “escape from Tokyo” feeling but more transit.  
Tag: `hiking`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,500 to 6,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 1,000 to 2,000`
  - Food, snacks, and other: `JPY 1,500 to 4,000`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `90 to 120 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `JR/train + bus/cable car + hiking/walking`
  - Rough route: go `far west` into the `Okutama/Mitake` side of Tokyo, use rail as the long leg, then transition to one mountain approach rather than trying to cover multiple valleys
- Map links:
  - [Mt. Mitake](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mount+Mitake)
  - [Mitake Gorge area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mitake+Gorge)

Suggested flow:
- Use the [official Okutama tourism site](https://okutama.gr.jp/site/) and [Mt. Mitake info](https://www.mitaketozan.co.jp/foreign/en/) to decide whether to do shrine-focused Mitake or a gorge walk.
- Keep this as a single-objective day; do not over-stack too many trail sections.

Opening-time considerations:
- This requires an earlier start than Takao.
- Best skipped in heavy rain or if trail surfaces may be icy.

Why this is a fit:
- Stronger wilderness feel than the easier Tokyo nature options.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: using a good weather window well and committing to one regional area instead of trying to combine too many side trips.
- Pacing tip: start earlier than on Tokyo city days and keep at least one part of the itinerary skippable in case transit, weather, or crowds slow you down.
- Easy adjustment: if conditions look poor in the morning, replace this with one of the indoor-heavy Tokyo plans and save the trip for a clearer day.
