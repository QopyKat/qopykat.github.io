# 38. Water Bus and Small Worlds Future-Tokyo Day

Short summary: A highly different day that mixes Tokyo-from-the-water with a miniature museum and a slightly surreal Ariake/Odaiba mood.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 4,000 to 9,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 1,000 to 2,000`
  - Food, tickets, and other: `JPY 3,000 to 7,000`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `25 to 45 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: `train/subway + water bus + indoor walking`
  - Rough route: start `east` at `Asakusa`, move `downriver/southeast` by water toward the bay, then finish in `Ariake/Odaiba` with minimal extra transfers
- Map links:
  - [Asakusa water bus pier](https://maps.google.com/?q=Tokyo+Cruise+Asakusa+Pier)
  - [SMALL WORLDS](https://maps.google.com/?q=SMALL+WORLDS+Tokyo)
  - [Ariake / Odaiba area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ariake+Tokyo)

Suggested flow:
- Start with the [Asakusa water bus / cruise context from GO TOKYO](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/story/walks-and-tours/waterfront/take-the-water-bus-from-asakusa-to-odaiba/index.html) and use the river-to-bay transition as the first half of the day.
- Pair that with [SMALL WORLDS Miniature Museum](https://smallworlds.jp/en/), which is large, indoor, and genuinely unusual by Tokyo standards.
- This day works best if you lean into the contrast between old-river Tokyo and futuristic/artificial waterfront Tokyo.
- End with an Ariake or Odaiba dinner if the weather is clear, or just head back once the indoor part is done.

Opening-time considerations:
- [SMALL WORLDS](https://smallworlds.jp/en/) currently lists `09:00 to 19:00`, with last admission around `18:00`.
- Water bus schedules are the most important timing constraint here; confirm the specific sailing you want before committing the rest of the route.

Why this is a fit:
- One of the most different-feeling days in the whole set.
- Good for bad weather if the cruise portion is still running and you want a mostly indoor backup after it.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: when you want one day in the trip to feel genuinely unusual or unlike standard first-time Tokyo sightseeing.
- Pacing tip: let the unusual anchor be the point of the day and resist overloading it with unrelated stops just to make the itinerary feel fuller.
- Easy adjustment: if the main anchor turns out to be shorter than expected, add a nearby cafe, neighborhood walk, or early dinner rather than rushing across the city.
