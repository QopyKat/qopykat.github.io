# 36. Meguro Parasite Museum and Weird-Curious Tokyo Day

Short summary: A deliberately unusual Tokyo day built around one of the city's strangest museums, with a calmer Meguro-area walk to balance it out.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,000 to 6,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 400 to 800`
  - Food, coffee, and other: `JPY 1,600 to 5,200`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `20 to 35 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + neighborhood walking`
  - Rough route: go `south/southwest` toward `Meguro`, do the museum anchor, then keep the rest of the day local around one or two nearby walks or cafe stops
- Map links:
  - [Meguro Parasitological Museum](https://maps.google.com/?q=Meguro+Parasitological+Museum)
  - [Meguro Fudoson / Ryusenji](https://maps.google.com/?q=Meguro+Fudoson)
  - [Meguro station area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Meguro+Station)

Suggested flow:
- Start with the [Meguro Parasitological Museum](https://www.kiseichu.org/e-top), which is one of the most unusual museum stops in Tokyo and genuinely different from the standard art/history circuit.
- Pair it with a low-pressure Meguro walk rather than another heavy attraction.
- Add [Meguro Fudoson / Ryusenji Temple](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/spot/471/index.html) or a coffee break so the day does not become only “the weird museum day.”
- Keep dinner flexible in Meguro, Ebisu, or Nakameguro depending on energy.

Opening-time considerations:
- The [Meguro Parasitological Museum official site](https://www.kiseichu.org/e-top) has its own calendar and should be checked directly before locking the date.
- This is a short-anchor day, so it works best paired with walking, cafes, and one extra neighborhood stop.

Why this is a fit:
- One of the clearest “that was different” experiences in Tokyo.
- Good if you like science, odd museums, or travel days that feel memorable rather than conventionally pretty.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: when you want one day in the trip to feel genuinely unusual or unlike standard first-time Tokyo sightseeing.
- Pacing tip: let the unusual anchor be the point of the day and resist overloading it with unrelated stops just to make the itinerary feel fuller.
- Easy adjustment: if the main anchor turns out to be shorter than expected, add a nearby cafe, neighborhood walk, or early dinner rather than rushing across the city.
