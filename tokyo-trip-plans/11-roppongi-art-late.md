# 11. Roppongi Art Late Day

Short summary: One of Tokyo's strongest indoor-heavy days thanks to museum density and later evening options.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 4,000 to 10,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 400 to 800`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 3,600 to 9,200`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 30 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway + short district walks`
  - Rough route: go `southwest` into `Roppongi`, stay concentrated between one or two museums and dinner/drinks nearby, then return without adding extra districts
- Map links:
  - [Mori Art Museum](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mori+Art+Museum)
  - [The National Art Center, Tokyo](https://maps.google.com/?q=The+National+Art+Center+Tokyo)

Suggested flow:
- Choose one primary museum:
  - [Mori Art Museum](https://www.mori.art.museum/en/)
  - [The National Art Center, Tokyo](https://www.nact.jp/english/)
- If energy is high, add [21_21 DESIGN SIGHT](https://www.2121designsight.jp/en/).
- Finish with dinner or drinks in Roppongi, Azabudai, or Nishi-Azabu rather than leaving immediately after the museum.

Opening-time considerations:
- Mori is especially useful because it often runs later than many other Tokyo museums.
- National Art Center timing depends on the specific exhibition, so confirm hours when you lock dates.

Why this is a fit:
- Excellent bad-weather day.
- Very strong for art, architecture, and nightlife without clubbing.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
