# 23. Kamakura and Enoshima Day

Short summary: One of the best day trips from Tokyo for temples, coastal air, walking, and food.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 8,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 1,200 to 2,400`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 1,800 to 5,600`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `75 to 110 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `JR or Odakyu/private railway + lots of walking`, with optional local `Enoden` segments
  - Rough route: go `south` toward the coast, center the day on `Kamakura`, and only extend farther west to `Enoshima` if weather, daylight, and energy still look good
- Map links:
  - [Kamakura station area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kamakura)
  - [Enoshima](https://maps.google.com/?q=Enoshima)

Suggested flow:
- Use the [Enoshima-Kamakura Freepass](https://www.odakyu.jp/english/passes/enoshima_kamakura/) if the route fits your base.
- Pick only two or three Kamakura anchors rather than trying to “do everything.”
- Add Enoshima only if weather is decent and daylight is sufficient.
- Good anchor ideas from [Japan-Guide's Kamakura overview](https://www.japan-guide.com/e/e2166.html).

Opening-time considerations:
- Start early; transit is longer and temple entry windows are daytime-oriented.
- This trip gets much better when not rushed.

Why this is a fit:
- Very high hit rate across culture, walking, and food.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: using a good weather window well and committing to one regional area instead of trying to combine too many side trips.
- Pacing tip: start earlier than on Tokyo city days and keep at least one part of the itinerary skippable in case transit, weather, or crowds slow you down.
- Easy adjustment: if conditions look poor in the morning, replace this with one of the indoor-heavy Tokyo plans and save the trip for a clearer day.
