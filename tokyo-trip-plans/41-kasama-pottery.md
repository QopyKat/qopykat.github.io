# 41. Kasama Pottery and Shrine Day

Short summary: A quieter Ibaraki escape for pottery, galleries, and an old shrine town instead of a more obvious mega-destination.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 8,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 1,500 to 3,000`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 1,500 to 5,000`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `90 to 120 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `JR/train + local bus/taxi + walking`
  - Rough route: travel `northeast` into `Kasama` in Ibaraki, keep the day centered on `pottery streets`, one `ceramic stop`, and `Kasama Inari Shrine`
- Map links:
  - [Kasama Inari Shrine](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kasama+Inari+Shrine)
  - [Ibaraki Ceramic Art Museum](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ibaraki+Ceramic+Art+Museum)

Suggested flow:
- This is directly inspired by Chris Broad's [How to Suck at Pottery](https://www.abroadinjapan.com/content-54-how-to-suck-at-pottery), which was filmed as a trip to Kasama in Ibaraki.
- Build the day around [Kasama Inari Shrine](https://visit.ibarakiguide.jp/en/sightseeing/22308/), the [Ibaraki Ceramic Art Museum](https://visit.ibarakiguide.jp/en/sightseeing/22291/), and either [Crafthills Kasama](https://visit.ibarakiguide.jp/en/sightseeing/22309/) or [Yakimono Dori / To no Komichi](https://visit.ibarakiguide.jp/en/sightseeing/41594/).
- If pottery shopping matters, keep extra bag space and avoid trying to add another city on the same day.

Opening-time considerations:
- Start earlier than on Tokyo city days because the transit is meaningfully longer.
- Museum and workshop hours matter more here than nightlife, so treat this as a daytime trip with a straightforward return.

Why this is a fit:
- It adds a genuinely Chris Broad-style side trip: specific, memorable, and less overexposed than the usual Tokyo orbit staples.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: people who like crafts, ceramics, and lower-stress regional wandering more than blockbuster sightseeing.
- Pacing tip: do one pottery-focused venue thoroughly rather than trying to hit every gallery and shop.
- Easy adjustment: if weather is poor, lean harder on the museum and indoor craft stops and shorten the shrine-area walk.
