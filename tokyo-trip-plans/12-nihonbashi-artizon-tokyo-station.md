# 12. Nihonbashi, Artizon, and Tokyo Station Design Day

Short summary: Historic merchant Tokyo mixed with one excellent museum and refined food-floor browsing.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 8,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 350 to 700`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 2,650 to 7,300`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 25 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway/train + compact walking`
  - Rough route: head `east` or `northeast` toward `Nihonbashi/Tokyo Station`, walk between museum, commercial streets, and station food halls with minimal transit once there
- Map links:
  - [Artizon Museum](https://maps.google.com/?q=Artizon+Museum)
  - [Nihonbashi](https://maps.google.com/?q=Nihonbashi)

Suggested flow:
- Start at [Artizon Museum](https://www.artizon.museum/en/).
- Walk Nihonbashi and the surrounding historical-commercial district using [GO TOKYO's Nihonbashi guide](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/destinations/central-tokyo/nihombashi/index.html).
- Finish around Tokyo Station for depachika food shopping or an early train back.

Opening-time considerations:
- Artizon is a timed, proper museum stop; do not leave it until evening.
- Department-store food floors are best late afternoon to early evening.

Why this is a fit:
- Smooth indoor option for cold weather.
- Easy to pair with food-focused browsing.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
