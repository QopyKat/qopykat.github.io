# 28. Christmas Eve Illuminations and Bars Day

Short summary: Tokyo does Christmas as a commercial/romantic city event, not a shutdown. Use that.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 5,000 to 14,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 400 to 900`
  - Food, drinks, and other: `JPY 4,600 to 13,100`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 35 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway/train + festive walking`
  - Rough route: pick one `central or southwest` illumination district such as `Roppongi`, `Marunouchi`, or `Ebisu`, and keep the whole evening clustered there
- Map links:
  - [Roppongi / Midtown area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Tokyo+Midtown)
  - [Ebisu Garden Place](https://maps.google.com/?q=Ebisu+Garden+Place)

Suggested flow:
- Book dinner early.
- Choose one decorated evening district such as Roppongi, Marunouchi, or Ebisu instead of trying to sample several.
- Keep the night mostly outdoors plus one warm indoor bar/cafe finish.

Opening-time considerations:
- Restaurants and seasonal venues can book out well before December 24.
- Expect busy commercial areas, but not the kind of citywide closure pattern common in some Western countries.

Why this is a fit:
- Best Christmas-specific Tokyo day.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: special dates when atmosphere matters more than maximizing attractions.
- Pacing tip: protect one reservation or one main holiday moment, then keep the rest of the day deliberately flexible.
- Easy adjustment: if closures or crowds become annoying, simplify the plan into one neighborhood, one meal booking, and one evening activity.
