# 15. Nakano Broadway and West-Tokyo Bars Day

Short summary: Vintage fandom shopping, retro Tokyo energy, and a good transition into a relaxed local-feeling night.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,500 to 7,500`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 450 to 900`
  - Food, drinks, and other: `JPY 2,050 to 6,600`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `20 to 30 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `JR/local train + walking`
  - Rough route: go `west` to `Nakano`, spend the day around `Broadway` and surrounding shotengai, then either stay in Nakano or continue a short local hop to Koenji for bars
- Map links:
  - [Nakano Broadway](https://maps.google.com/?q=Nakano+Broadway)
  - [Nakano station area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Nakano+Tokyo)

Suggested flow:
- Put [Nakano Broadway](https://nakano-broadway.com/) at the center of the day.
- Browse the side streets and shotengai outside the complex rather than treating it as a single-building stop.
- Keep dinner and drinks local in Nakano or shift onward to Koenji if you want a livelier bar finish.

Opening-time considerations:
- Nakano Broadway works best from late morning or lunch onward.
- Many specialist shops open later than museums, so do not over-start this day.

Why this is a fit:
- High-value anime/game collecting day with a more local-feeling finish than central Tokyo.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
