# 24. Yokohama Bayside Urban Day

Short summary: A different large-city mood from Tokyo with waterfront walking, architecture, and easy food options.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,500 to 7,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 800 to 1,600`
  - Food, admissions, and other: `JPY 1,700 to 5,400`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `35 to 55 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `JR/private railway + waterfront walking`
  - Rough route: head `south` to `Yokohama`, then stay inside the `Minato Mirai/Red Brick/Chinatown` belt rather than zig-zagging all over the city
- Map links:
  - [Minato Mirai](https://maps.google.com/?q=Minato+Mirai)
  - [Yokohama Chinatown](https://maps.google.com/?q=Yokohama+Chinatown)

Suggested flow:
- Base the route on [Yokohama Official Visitors Guide](https://www.yokohamajapan.com/).
- Focus on Minato Mirai, Red Brick Warehouse, and Chinatown rather than scattering too widely.
- Strong evening skyline value if the weather is clear.

Opening-time considerations:
- This is flexible enough for a later start than Kamakura or Nikko.
- Keep waterfront walking for daylight or blue hour.

Why this is a fit:
- Easy, low-stress day trip with strong food payoff.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: using a good weather window well and committing to one regional area instead of trying to combine too many side trips.
- Pacing tip: start earlier than on Tokyo city days and keep at least one part of the itinerary skippable in case transit, weather, or crowds slow you down.
- Easy adjustment: if conditions look poor in the morning, replace this with one of the indoor-heavy Tokyo plans and save the trip for a clearer day.
