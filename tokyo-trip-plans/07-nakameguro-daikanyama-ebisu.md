# 07. Nakameguro, Daikanyama, and Ebisu Day

Short summary: Stylish but still walkable neighborhoods with river views, bookstores, design, and great dinner options.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 4,000 to 10,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 400 to 800`
  - Food, drinks, and other: `JPY 3,600 to 9,200`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 30 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + uphill/downhill walking`
  - Rough route: head `southwest` to `Nakameguro`, walk uphill into `Daikanyama`, then drift or short-hop into `Ebisu` for the evening finish
- Map links:
  - [Nakameguro](https://maps.google.com/?q=Nakameguro)
  - [Daikanyama T-Site](https://maps.google.com/?q=Daikanyama+T-Site)

Suggested flow:
- Start with a relaxed walk in Nakameguro using the [GO TOKYO area guide](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/destinations/southern-tokyo/nakameguro/).
- Walk uphill to [Daikanyama T-Site](https://store.tsite.jp/daikanyama/english/) and nearby design-heavy streets.
- End in Ebisu for dinner, drinks, or [YEBISU BREWERY TOKYO](https://www.sapporobeer.jp/brewery/y_museum/).

Opening-time considerations:
- Daikanyama stores are better from late morning onward.
- Brewery/gallery visits should be done before dinner service ramps up.

Why this is a fit:
- Great all-weather urban day with high food quality and a relaxed pace.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
