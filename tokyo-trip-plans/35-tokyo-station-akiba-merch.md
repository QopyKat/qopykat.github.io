# 35. Tokyo Station Character Street and Akiba Merch Day

Short summary: A merch-heavy route that starts polished and official at Tokyo Station, then shifts into denser collector energy in Akihabara.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 3,000 to 8,500` before shopping
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 350 to 800`
  - Food, cafes, and other: `JPY 2,650 to 7,700` before shopping
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 30 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `train/subway + short JR hop or one local transfer + walking`
  - Rough route: start `east/central` around `Tokyo Station`, then make one short move `north` to `Akihabara` for the second half rather than ping-ponging between areas
- Map links:
  - [First Avenue Tokyo Station / Character Street](https://maps.google.com/?q=First+Avenue+Tokyo+Station)
  - [Akihabara Radio Kaikan](https://maps.google.com/?q=Akihabara+Radio+Kaikan)
  - [Akihabara Station area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Akihabara+Station)

Suggested flow:
- Start at [First Avenue Tokyo Station](https://www.tokyoeki-1bangai.co.jp/en/), with [Tokyo Character Street](https://www.tokyoeki-1bangai.co.jp/tokyocharacterstreet/) as the main opening block.
- This works especially well if you like official-shop energy, station exclusives, and a broad spread of character brands in one place.
- Move to Akihabara in the afternoon and anchor the second half in [Akihabara Radio Kaikan](https://akihabara-radiokaikan.co.jp/), whose building directory includes stores like AmiAmi, K-BOOKS, Lashinbang, Kaiyodo, and Yellow Submarine.
- Use the Tokyo Station side for cleaner official merch and the Akihabara side for secondhand, figures, hobby goods, and harder-to-predict finds.

Opening-time considerations:
- The English [First Avenue Tokyo Station guide](https://www.tokyoeki-1bangai.co.jp/en/) lists Tokyo Character Street as `10:00 to 20:30`.
- [Akihabara Radio Kaikan](https://akihabara-radiokaikan.co.jp/access/) lists general building hours as `10:00 to 20:00`.
- This is best as a full daytime plan rather than a late-start evening browse.

Why this is a fit:
- Good if you want one anime/game merch day that is broader than a single franchise or single district.
- One of the best plans for people who enjoy comparing official retail with resale/hobby store stock.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: fandom-heavy days where shopping, pop-ups, and current collabs are a real part of the fun rather than an afterthought.
- Pacing tip: set a budget ceiling early, because these days are easy to extend by hours once you start finding stores or limited goods you like.
- Easy adjustment: if the current event or merch situation looks weak, keep the area but pivot toward cafes, arcades, or one neighboring district instead.
