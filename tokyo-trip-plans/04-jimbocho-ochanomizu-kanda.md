# 04. Jimbocho, Ochanomizu, and Kanda Day

Short summary: Used bookstores, music shops, curry, shrine culture, and low-key old-Tokyo urban texture.  
Tag: `strong fit`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,500 to 6,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 350 to 700`
  - Food, coffee, and other: `JPY 2,150 to 5,300`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `15 to 25 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway/train + walking`
  - Rough route: head `north` into the `Jimbocho/Ochanomizu/Kanda` belt, walk the corridor from books to music stores to shrine, then optionally tack on a short final hop toward Akihabara
- Map links:
  - [Jimbocho Book Town](https://maps.google.com/?q=Jimbocho+Book+Town)
  - [Kanda Myojin](https://maps.google.com/?q=Kanda+Myojin)

Suggested flow:
- Start in [Jimbocho Book Town](https://visit-chiyoda.tokyo/app/spot/detail/333) for used books, coffee, and curry.
- Walk north through Ochanomizu instrument streets.
- Continue to [Kanda Myojin](https://www.kandamyoujin.or.jp/en/) for a culture/anime crossover stop.
- If energy is still high, finish with a short Akihabara detour without making it the whole day.

Opening-time considerations:
- Bookstores and specialty shops often open late morning, so this is not a dawn-start day.
- Shrine visit is flexible; retail browsing is the timing-sensitive part.

Why this is a fit:
- Excellent for games/anime-adjacent culture without spending the whole day in loud arcades.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
