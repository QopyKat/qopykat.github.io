# 20. Shibamata and East-Side Old Tokyo Day

Short summary: Slower-paced retro Tokyo with temple-town energy and fewer visitors than the central classics.  
Tag: `experimental`

- Estimated total cost per person: `JPY 2,000 to 5,000`
- Cost breakdown:
  - Transit per person: `JPY 600 to 1,100`
  - Food, snacks, and other: `JPY 1,400 to 3,900`
- Estimated transit time from central Tokyo base: `35 to 55 min each way`
- Transit breakdown:
  - Mode mix: mostly `subway/train + local private line + walking`
  - Rough route: go `east/northeast` toward the `Katsushika` side, arrive in `Shibamata`, and keep the rest of the day entirely walkable around the temple street and river edge
- Map links:
  - [Shibamata Taishakuten](https://maps.google.com/?q=Shibamata+Taishakuten)
  - [Shibamata station area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Shibamata)

Suggested flow:
- Walk the approach street and visit [Shibamata Taishakuten Temple](https://www.taishakuten.com/en).
- Keep this as a half-city, half-rest day with snack stops and low-pressure wandering.
- If desired, add a short river walk rather than another heavy sightseeing block.

Opening-time considerations:
- Most value here is daytime; this is not a nightlife-heavy choice.
- Best fitted into a lighter day or after a late previous night.

Why this is a fit:
- Quiet, old-Tokyo atmosphere with almost no overplanning needed.

## Extra planning notes

- Best for: city days where you want one clear anchor plus plenty of room for food, coffee, or unplanned browsing.
- Pacing tip: this plan works well if you keep one major stop fixed and treat the rest as optional rather than trying to maximize every block.
- Easy adjustment: if energy drops or the weather shifts, shorten the walking portion and turn the day into one anchor activity plus a better dinner or cafe finish.
